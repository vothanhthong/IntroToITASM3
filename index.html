1. Cloud Computing

Simply put, cloud computing is the delivery of computing services - this includes servers, storage, databases, networking, software, intelligence, and analytics – over “the cloud” (the internet) to increase flexibility and scalability. Cloud computing in general offers remote access to any work-related data, an example of this is how programmers can save their codes online in GitHub. Cloud adoption has seen “a sharp rise” (Kreigh, 2021, p. 111) during the height of 2020’s Corona-induced pandemic. Providers are currently researching on how to efficiently use cloud computing to deal with the numerous machine-to-machine communication, data, process, etc. The main enabling technology for cloud computing is virtualization. Virtualization software works as a separator. It divides one single –physical- computing device into one or more virtual devices, each of which can be easily manipulated to perform computing tasks. This in turn, enables the providers to virtualize servers, storages and other physical hardware or data center resources.

From an objective point of view, cloud computing is a young field, with exponential potential growth. Cloud computing itself is very versatile, enables many different directions to take. With that being said, cloud computing is undoubtedly impactful at the very least to society as it had increased the competitiveness of IT services available to the public and private alike. This in turn creates a substantial number of opportunities leading to new services (more jobs), which further prove the versatility of cloud computing as a job and as a field itself. as long as there is internet connection, you can always use cloud services (either for storage or computing), so cloud computing affects us all as a whole rather than a niche in society.

From a subjective point of view however, cloud computing had been a big help for me, especially since I am a programmer, and to be able to save your codes online is a luxury. I have always been used to cloud computing so my life is mostly unaffected with it, but without it, life can be quite troublesome. Cloud computing helps people stay in contact with each other, for example my friends and I, follow events or news, etc.

2. Cybersecurity and privacy

Cybersecurity, in its simplest term, is the practice of protecting programs, systems, and networks from digital attacks. Cybersecurity is a volatile field, with an “average 270 attacks per company over the year, a 31% increase over 2020. Third-party risk continues to dominate, with successful breaches to the organization through the supply chain have increased from 44% to 61%” (D.H.James, 2020, p. 11). Cybersecurity ensures the privacy of users remain, “private”, this includes several actions to ensure minimum breach: prevention of unauthorized data/personnel; regulatory compliance; regular intervals of verifying data’s integrity; etc. The logarithmic growth rate of Cybersecurity has led many people to believe that AI will soon be the “core component of all cybersecurity, be it offline or online, systems” (D.H.James, 2020, p. 99). Cybersecurity was “invented” in the 1970s, when two men – Bob “Creeper” Thomas and Ray “Reaper” Tomlinson – created two different programs – Creeper and Reaper respectively – to move over a network. Mr. Bob discovered that when a computer program moves over a network, it leaves behind a trail, he then created Creeper, so that the trail printed out a simple message “I’M THE CREEPER: CATCH ME IF YOU CAN.” This concerned a lot of people, including leading programming specialist of MIT – Ray Tomlinson- Ray then created Reaper for the sole purpose of catching and destroying Creeper.

Cybersecurity prevents malicious actions (hacking) to be taken place in users’ devices. This in turns pushed the “hackers” to grow as a community, this also led to the inevitable growth of technologies in general. the growth of the black hat community will naturally lead to the growth of the cybersecurity community. This contrasting relationship is the fundamental push for the never-ending growth of technologies. Cybersecurity requires new services to be needed, therefore creating more jobs and opportunities to grow.

The existence of cybersecurity serves not only as a beacon of “security”, but also a symbol of “changes”. Cybersecurity provides many people, including my family and I, online security, where almost all the people you’ve met or will eventually meet are strangers with different agendas. Personally, it had helped me and people I know going through my and their daily life, respectively, without worrying about being hacked or breached by random hackers.

3. Autonomous vehicle

Autonomous vehicle, with a self-explanatory name, create and maintain a map of their surroundings based on a variety of sensors situated in different parts of the vehicle. Radar sensors monitor the position of nearby vehicles; video cameras detect traffic lights, read road signs, track other vehicles, and look for pedestrians and so on. This technology has been in development since the 1920s, with the first full-fledged autonomous vehicle – the AutoMobile – appeared around 1980s. There is a guiding framework of standards for measuring autonomous vehicle capabilities, with five levels of vehicle autonomy as defined by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE). They range from human control (level 0 autonomy) to full autonomous (level 5 autonomy). Tesla’s pilot, for example, is considered level 2 autonomy, which means a driver is still needed to takeover; while the Waymo project from Google operates at level 4 autonomy, meaning it is only 1 step away from being a true autonomous vehicle. Self-driving vehicles employ a wide range of technologies like radar, cameras, ultrasound, radio antennas, etc. to navigate safely on the roads.

Autonomous has left behind massive impacts upon the job market, the general business, the health system and many more systems alike. Autonomous vehicle, like many other areas in IT, is growing exponentially. We’re expecting Tesla’s pilot, for example, to reach level 3 autonomy by the end of 2025. Autonomous vehicle had and will probably affect many jobs/fields. Autonomous vehicles can bring the end of truck drivers, taxi drivers, and so on, as there is not a need for a physical driver in order to takeover.

Autonomous vehicle in general is not a big part of my life or my family. Soon, once the technology has been improved upon itself, I may just acquire it, purely out of curiosity.

4. Chatbots

Chatbot is a computer program that stimulates a real life, human – hopefully natural – conversation. Users can interact with said chatbots via the chat interface or voice input. There are currently 3 main types. The simplest and probably the first type of chatbot invented - the rigid, multiple choices, rule-based chatbots. The others are more complicated. The Intellectually Independent Chatbots use machine learning to help them learn from users’ specific inputs and request – machine learning helps the bots recognize certain keywords that triggers certain replies, with the bots getting better and better overtime. The last and latest chatbots, which combined the best of both worlds, namely the rule-based bots and the machine learning bots, is called AI-powered chatbots. these latest models understand all languages, with their local memory works similarly like a cookie, saving both the context and the user’s preferences. These chatbots can also jump from one point of conversation scenario to another when needed and address random user request at any moment. In order to achieve this, these AI-Powered chatbots use a combination of machine learning, AI, and Natural Language Processing. NLP is the ability of the computer to understand and analyze human speech, to understand the inputted human language, therefore, find the right response and reply correctly to said human language. They have been widely considered as the “future of business communication”. This can be because businesses can now easily integrate chatbots into their social media channels to build customer relationships. This in turn, creates brand awareness and automate purchase processes, which can be very time and wit-consuming. With chatbots getting smarter the more they talk, thanks to AI and deep learning, said processes had become much simpler.

Chatbots are important, especially to the people of the business world, where they are regarding it as the primary channel for marketing conversations. Since you can have chatbots talking to the users, without the need to pay for someone doing the job, chatbots had also negatively impacted society as “jobs regarding marketing have seen a sharp drop in the last quarter of 2014 (total of -11%).” (J.D.Quagmire, 2019). Chatbots will get smarter and better overtime, because it can improve upon itself during real-time conversations.

Chatbots were not a big part of my experience growing up, neither was it present in my family throughout the years. I can only say chatbots, in its purest form, has helped many people overcome many things, or at the very least, had helped many businesses bloomed.
